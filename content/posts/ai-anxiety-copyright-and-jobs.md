Couldn't sleep well last night. Was scrolling through Twitter and Reddit, and honestly, the sheer volume of AI copyright lawsuits and "AGI is taking your job tomorrow" posts just got under my skin.

I usually try to ignore the hype cycle. Just close the tab, open VS Code, and build my stuff. But recently, it's harder to tune out. Seeing indie devs and artists get their entire portfolios scraped to train models that will eventually compete with them... it just sucks. There's this heavy, unspoken question hanging over every side project I start lately: *Am I just generating free training data for OpenAI or Anthropic?*

It’s a weird kind of grief. You spend years getting good at something—debugging, writing, designing—and then you watch a prompt bar do 80% of it in three seconds. And sure, the output is often generic or slightly broken, but it's *fast*, and for most companies, "fast and cheap" beats "crafted with care."

I caught myself staring at a blank markdown file for an hour today. What’s the point of writing if an AI can summarize it better than I can write it? What’s the point of coding this feature if Cursor could probably spit it out while I make coffee?

I don't know. The tech bros keep saying "adapt or die" or "AI won't replace you, a person using AI will." It feels so hollow. Like we're supposed to just enthusiastically cheer while the ground shifts under our feet.

I'm not anti-AI. I use it every day. But the anxiety is real. I think I just need to step away from the timeline for a few days. Maybe focus on things algorithms can't do yet—like going for a walk, or writing messy, unoptimized diary entries like this one. 

Just taking a breath. We'll see how things look tomorrow.
